No monthly billing — QRFY offers quarterly, bi-annual, and annual terms only. The shortest commitment is a quarterly plan at ~$41/month billed ~€123 every 3 months (~$492 over 12 months); annual is ~$20/month ($240/year, ~$720 over 3 years) paid upfront. Unlimited dynamic QR codes on all paid plans. No lifetime deal.

## Free Tier

No standing free tier for dynamic codes: sign-up starts a 7-day trial that creates dynamic QR codes by default; when the trial ends the codes deactivate, and reactivation requires a paid subscription with a minimum 3-month quarterly commitment (~$41/month).

## Pros

- Unlimited dynamic QR codes on all paid plans — a cost advantage at high volume that the articles concede Pageloot does not match.
- Supports 25+ QR code types, versus Pageloot's 15.
- 4.6-star G2 rating (221 reviews), with users praising the unlimited codes and easy interface.
- Customer support is QRFY's most-mentioned positive on G2 (66 mentions), with some reviewers describing responsive, compassionate service.
- Includes API access and bulk CSV creation (500 codes per CSV) on paid plans.
- Annual plan at ~$20/month is inexpensive for unlimited codes, and the platform has a large market presence (10,808 Trustpilot reviews).

## Cons

- Sign-up creates dynamic QR codes by default that deactivate after 7 days — users often discover this after printing materials (business cards, wedding invitations, funeral programs, flyers, resumes).
- No monthly billing — the shortest commitment is a quarterly plan billed ~€123 per 3 months (~$41/month).
- Multiple reviewers report auto-renewal without clear notice (the Reddit roundup cites surprise charges of $120–$287) and cancellation emails being ignored.
- Reported refund denials, including within the EU's 14-day cooling-off period, with some users resorting to bank chargebacks.
- GDPR compliance only — no SOC2, HIPAA, or ISO 27001 certifications, no SSO/SAML, and the app and landing pages are English only.
- No lifetime deal and no agency features: no shareable client reports, white-label dashboard, UTM tracking, custom table views, or print templates.

## Key Features

- Unlimited dynamic QR codes on all paid plans
- 25+ QR code types (menus, coupons, social media profiles, Wi-Fi, events, and more)
- Custom (no-code) landing pages
- Scan analytics with city-level tracking
- Bulk creation via CSV on paid plans (500 per CSV)
- API access on paid plans
- User and role management (multi-user seats)
- Any file upload as QR (documents, multimedia)
- Google Pixel + Facebook Pixel integration (as stated in the head-to-head comparison table)
- Basic folders for organization
- Custom domain support (mentioned only in the marketing-teams roundup)
- GDPR compliance (no SOC2, HIPAA, or ISO 27001)

## Watch-outs

The most-documented complaint: the 7-day trial creates dynamic codes by default and deactivates them when it ends, with reactivation requiring a paid subscription at a minimum 3-month quarterly commitment (~$41/month, ~€123 — one head-to-head passage says ~€150).

Reviewer reports across Trustpilot, Product Hunt, and SourceForge (per the articles):

- Auto-renewal without clear notice.
- Cancellation emails ignored — one Trustpilot reviewer was charged three extra billing cycles and disputed via credit card.
- Refund denials even within the EU 14-day cooling-off window.

Feature gaps: city-level-only scan tracking, single PDF per QR code, no audio/MP3 QR codes, no Smart Rules (conditional QR).

The articles advise documenting any cancellation request in writing via multiple channels.

## Notes

- **Source caveat:** all three source articles are published by Pageloot, a direct competitor, and the head-to-head explicitly positions Pageloot as the alternative (14-day vs 7-day trial, monthly billing, 30-day refund, $197 lifetime deal, agency features) — treat comparative judgments accordingly.
- **Pageloot's framing, attributed:** Pageloot contrasts QRFY's strong G2 rating with more negative reviews on Product Hunt, SourceForge, PissedConsumer, and parts of Trustpilot. Its own FAQ states QRFY "is not a scam," and it concedes QRFY wins on unlimited codes, QR type count, and API access — noting the 7-day dynamic-code expiry pattern is not unique to QRFY.
- **Facts:** QRFY is a Spanish company (qrfy.com).
- **Conflicts found:** the head-to-head internally conflicts on the quarterly total (~€150 in one passage vs ~€123 in its own table, FAQ, and both roundups — ~€123 kept as primary); page-3 gives the G2 score without a review count (221 taken from the other two articles).
- **Rating snapshots:** the marketing roundup and head-to-head cite April 2026 data; the Reddit roundup cites June 2026 — the numbers are identical across all three.
