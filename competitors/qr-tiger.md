Four recurring subscription tiers: Regular $7/mo ($84/yr), Advanced $16/mo ($192/yr), Premium $37/mo ($444/yr), Business $89/mo ($1,068/yr per the marketing roundup). Monthly billing exists only on the Regular plan — all higher tiers are annual-only. There is no lifetime/one-time option; 3-year cost on Regular is $252. The refund policy is listed only as "standard" in the head-to-head comparison table.

## Free Tier

Free static QR codes with no signup (Reddit roundup feature matrix). Dynamic codes are available only via a free trial/plan with a scan cap — roughly 100 scans per year per the Reddit roundup, after which trial codes stop working. The head-to-head paraphrases a G2 reviewer who hit the free plan's scan limit after printing thousands of hotel labels.

## Pros

- Rated 4.8/5 on both G2 (145 reviews) and Trustpilot (158 reviews) — the strongest review scores among paid tools in these comparisons.
- Multi-URL conditional routing sends one QR code to different destinations by scan location, device type, or time of day.
- Supports GS1 Digital Link for retail/CPG product packaging and has a native Canva integration for design workflows.
- Broad integration stack: native HubSpot and Zapier, GA4/GTM/Meta Pixel tracking, and API access.
- Bulk generation handles up to 3,000 codes per CSV upload, with 20+ dynamic QR code types.
- 24/7 support is consistently praised in reviews ("quick," "professional," "helpful"), and the company (est. 2018) claims 850,000+ brands across 147 countries including Disney, Hilton, Samsung, Uber, and TikTok.

## Cons

- No shareable client reports, no built-in UTM tracking, no custom table views, and no print templates.
- White-label is enterprise-only with custom pricing, so QR Tiger branding is visible to clients on standard plans.
- Monthly billing only on the $7 Regular plan — Advanced, Premium, and Business are annual-only, and no lifetime/one-time purchase exists.
- QR code designs cannot be modified after creation; visual changes require recreating the code entirely (G2, 3 mentions).
- Single PDF per QR code (no multi-PDF), no audio/MP3 QR codes, and scan tracking is city-level only (no state-level).
- GDPR-compliant only — no SOC2, HIPAA, or ISO 27001 certifications — and the refund policy is described only as "standard."

## Key Features

- Multi-URL conditional routing (different destinations by scan location, device type, or time of day)
- GS1 Digital Link support for product-level QR codes in retail/CPG packaging
- Native Canva integration (embed dynamic QR codes in Canva designs)
- Bulk generation up to 3,000 codes in a single CSV upload
- Native HubSpot and Zapier integrations
- GA4 + GTM + Meta Pixel tracking integrations
- 20+ dynamic QR code types
- API access
- Multi-user seats on higher plans
- 24/7 customer support
- Basic organization (tags/folders)

## Watch-outs

- **Annual lock-in above the entry tier:** monthly billing exists only on the $7 Regular plan, while Advanced ($16/mo), Premium ($37/mo), and Business ($89/mo) must be paid annually.
- **No lifetime option** — 3 years on Regular costs $252.
- **Scan-capped free/trial dynamic codes:** roughly 100 scans/yr per the Reddit roundup, and codes stop working afterward — though both roundups rate its trial-trap risk "Low".
- **Designs cannot be edited after creation** without recreating the code.

## Notes

- All three sources are published by Pageloot, a direct competitor — head-to-head claims below are attributed to Pageloot, not treated as fact.
- Pageloot's framing: it calls QR Tiger "a good product" and "not a takedown", "arguably the most feature-complete platform in this comparison" for individual marketers, and "Reddit's feature favorite" among paid tools. It explicitly recommends choosing QR Tiger over Pageloot for conditional routing, GS1, Canva/HubSpot, or API access today, while positioning it as built for individual marketers rather than agencies (Pageloot's own niche).
- The Reddit roundup adds that Redditors "defend it on the merits" (solid UI, fast support, deep features), with the recurring gripe being scan limits on lower tiers.
- Conflicts resolved in favor of the head-to-head article:
  1. **Compliance** — the Reddit roundup's feature matrix marks QR Tiger's SOC2/HIPAA/ISO cell as partial "ISO", but the head-to-head states GDPR only, with explicitly no SOC2, HIPAA, or ISO 27001.
  2. **Regular plan limits** — the marketing roundup says Regular is "capped at limited scans and codes", but the head-to-head pricing table says "12 dynamic codes, unlimited scans".
- Other verified facts: founded 2018; no ads injected on scans (Reddit roundup matrix); also operates MENU TIGER, a restaurant QR menu ordering system.
