Dynamic-QR plans start at $7/mo billed annually (Starter, $84/yr), with Premium at $12/mo billed annually ($144/yr) and Pro at $36/mo billed annually ($432/yr); monthly billing is available on all plans from $30/mo. One-time lifetime deals at $197 (Starter), $297 (Premium), and $897 (Pro) keep dynamic codes active forever. All plans come with a 14-day clearly labeled trial and a 30-day no-questions-asked worldwide refund.

## Free Tier

Free static QR code generator at `/qr-code-generator/` — no signup, no payment, no expiry, no ads, with free JPG/PNG and SVG downloads. Dynamic codes require a paid plan after a clearly labeled 14-day trial (double the 7 days at QR.io/QRFY). The Reddit roundup article additionally describes free hosted PDF/link/Google Maps QR tools where the code stays active permanently and basic scan counts are free, with detailed analytics and post-print PDF swapping as paid upgrades.

## Pros

- Lifetime deals ($197/$297/$897 one-time) keep dynamic codes active permanently with no recurring charges — a purchase option none of the compared competitors offer.
- Agency-specific features (shareable client reports, white-label dashboard, built-in UTM tracking, custom table views, print templates) on plans starting at $7/mo billed annually, where competitors gate them behind $250–399/mo tiers or don't offer them.
- Monthly billing is available on all plans (from $30/mo) and every plan carries a 30-day no-questions-asked worldwide refund.
- Free static QR generator with no signup and no expiry, and no ads injected between scan and content on any plan.
- 14-day clearly labeled dynamic-code trial — double the 7-day windows at QR.io and QRFY that the articles call trial traps.
- Strong multilingual support (37 landing-page languages, 16 dashboard languages) with live chat plus same-day email support on all plans, including weekends.

## Cons

- No public API, no Smart Rules/conditional routing, and no native Canva or HubSpot/CRM integrations yet — all listed as "in progress" while QR Tiger, Uniqode, QRFY, and Flowcode ship them today.
- No SOC2, HIPAA, or ISO 27001 certifications and no SSO/SAML — the articles themselves point enterprise compliance buyers to Uniqode.
- Only 15 dynamic QR types versus 20–46+ at competitors, with no GS1 Digital Link support for product packaging.
- Plan-based dynamic-code limits — Starter allows only 2 dynamic codes, versus unlimited codes on QRFY's paid plans.
- The advertised $7/mo entry price requires annual billing ($84/yr up front); true monthly billing starts at $30/mo.
- Smaller, younger market presence (est. 2019, 20,000+ brands in 110 countries) than incumbents like QR Tiger (850,000+ claimed) or Uniqode (50,000+ businesses), and no digital business card product, phone support, or formal uptime SLA.

## Key Features

- 15 dynamic QR code types, editable after printing, including multi-PDF, multi-MP3 audio, and any-file-upload codes
- Shareable branded client reports — live stats pages clients check without a login
- White-label dashboard (agency branding replaces Pageloot's)
- Built-in UTM tracking that auto-appends parameters at QR creation
- Custom table views (show/hide/reorder columns) plus folders, tags, and notes for organizing hundreds of codes
- Print templates for flyers, table stands, and business cards (built-in or upload your own)
- Scan analytics with state- and city-level tracking, device and time data, and full retention on all plans
- Meta Pixel and Google Analytics integration on all plans
- Multi-link landing pages in 37 languages; dashboard/app in 16 languages
- Bulk creation via CSV and full data export of all codes, contents, and URLs
- Multi-user seats available as an add-on from any plan
- Free static QR generator plus free link shortener, QR scanner, and barcode reader tools
- Live chat and same-day email support on all plans, including weekends
- 50% recurring affiliate commissions
- Lifetime deal option ($197/$297/$897 one-time) with codes active forever

## Watch-outs

- Dynamic codes deactivate without an active plan (like every dynamic-QR platform) unless on a lifetime deal.
- The headline $7/mo requires annual billing; month-to-month starts at $30/mo.
- Starter is capped at 2 dynamic codes, and all plans have plan-based limits.
- Several compared capabilities (API, Smart Rules, CRM integrations, SOC2/HIPAA/ISO 27001, password-protected codes, custom domains) are "in progress" or "coming soon", not shipped — buyers needing them today are out of luck.

## Notes

- **First-party source warning:** all 11 source articles are published by Pageloot itself on pageloot.com — this record IS Pageloot describing itself, so every claim is first-party marketing. The two roundups rank Pageloot #1 with a disclosure, and the head-to-heads are its own comparison landing pages (though they include "when the competitor is better" sections and self-criticism).

- **Best for:** marketing agencies and teams managing roughly 10–50 client brands (restaurants, e-commerce, local businesses) who need client-facing reports, white-label deliverables, UTM attribution, print templates, and monthly billing flexibility — plus anyone wanting permanent code ownership via the lifetime deal or free static codes without signup. Not a fit for enterprise compliance, API-driven, or GS1 packaging use cases (the articles themselves recommend Uniqode or QR Tiger there).
