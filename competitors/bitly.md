Free plan: 2 QR codes/month with unlimited scans, but destination editing is paywalled and ads run on free QR scans. Cheapest editable-QR plan is Core at $10/mo, annual billing only ($120/yr minimum). First monthly-billed tier is Growth at $35/mo ($29/mo annual). Premium is $199/mo annual or $300/mo monthly and is required for city-level and device analytics. QR codes are metered monthly: 2 (Free) / 5 (Core) / 10 (Growth) / 200 (Premium). Year-1 cost on Core is $120; 3-year cost $360. No lifetime deal — subscriptions only.

## Free Tier

2 QR codes per month with unlimited scans. Changing a code's destination ("Link & QR Code redirects") is gated behind paid plans, so free codes are static in everything but name. Bitly recently added advertising on free QR code scans — scanners may see a Bitly-served ad before reaching the destination.

## Pros

- Industry-leading link management — custom domains, branded short links, and link analytics are where the sources say Bitly genuinely excels.
- 4.5/5 G2 rating across 916 reviews, which the sources acknowledge as earned for link shortening.
- Holds SOC 2 Type 2, GDPR, and CCPA compliance certifications.
- Established household brand since 2008, with links, QR, and landing pages in one platform, reducing tool sprawl.
- Free-plan QR codes come with unlimited scans.
- UTM builder, API, and link-in-bio pages are built in.

## Cons

- Free QR codes can't be edited — changing the destination URL ("redirects") requires a paid plan, making free codes effectively static.
- Bitly serves ads on free QR code scans (a recent addition).
- Cheapest plan (Core, $10/mo) is annual-billing only at $120/yr minimum; monthly billing starts at Growth for $35/mo.
- QR codes are metered monthly (2/5/10/200 by plan) and QR is a secondary feature — no multi-PDF codes, no audio QR, no print templates, no shareable client reports or white-label dashboard.
- City-level and device analytics are gated behind Premium at $199–300/mo, and data retention is capped: 30 days on Core, 4 months on Growth (per the tables), 1 year on Premium.
- Trustpilot rating is 2.3/5 across 694 reviews, and there is no lifetime deal — recurring subscriptions only.

## Key Features

- Link shortening as the core product, with custom branded domains on Growth+ plans
- QR codes metered monthly by plan: 2 (Free) / 5 (Core) / 10 (Growth) / 200 (Premium)
- UTM builder included (marked as included in the feature tables of all three source articles)
- API (per the Reddit-roundup feature matrix)
- Built-in link-in-bio pages and landing pages in a unified dashboard with links and QR
- City-level scan tracking listed on Growth+ in one table row; city-level plus device-type analytics stated as Premium-only ($199–300/mo) in the body text
- SOC 2 Type 2, GDPR, and CCPA compliance certifications
- Meta Pixel and Google Analytics support
- Link export for migrating data out
- English-only landing pages and dashboard

## Watch-outs

- Core is annual-only ($120/yr upfront).
- Free QR destinations can't be edited, and free scans carry Bitly ads.
- QR codes are metered monthly by plan.
- Analytics retention caps (30 days Core / 4 months Growth per the tables / 1 year Premium) undermine month-over-month or year-over-year reporting.
- Pageloot's roundups rate Bitly's "trial trap risk" as Medium.
- Pageloot also relays Reddit warnings about Bitly's separate dedicated QR product (qrco.de): users describe auto-enrollment into $200+ annual renewals with refunds refused — this concerns qrco.de, not the main Bitly platform.

## Notes

- All three sources are published by Pageloot, a direct QR-platform competitor, and the head-to-head page is an explicit sales comparison — subjective framing here is attributed.
- Pageloot's core framing: "Bitly is a great link shortener. It's not a great QR code platform," and the Reddit roundup's consensus label is "QR is an afterthought."
- Pageloot claims Bitly's ownership of QR Code Generator Pro (Egoditor GmbH, 1.5/5 Trustpilot across 9,220 reviews; Egoditor also acquired QRCode Monkey in Feb 2024) shows a QR strategy of "acquire rather than build," and calls ads on free scans "a dealbreaker" for client-facing use.
- Paraphrased anecdotes relayed by Pageloot: a G2 reviewer reported previously-free features removed, free links opted into paid ad clickthroughs, and no human support without a paid plan; a Capterra reviewer's dead-code-after-14-days complaint concerns the subsidiary QR Code Generator Pro, not Bitly itself.
- Source conflicts: the head-to-head contradicts itself on Growth analytics retention (4 months in body/table — matching the roundup — vs 1 year in its FAQ), on UTM ("UTM builder included" in its table vs "no UTM tracking built in" in a bullet; all three articles' feature tables mark UTM as included, so it is kept here), on city-level tracking (Growth+ in one table row vs Premium-only in body text), and on the subsidiary's review count (9,220 vs 9,194).
- Founded 2008 in New York per the head-to-head.
- Pageloot discloses it researched Bitly via G2, Capterra, and Bitly's public pricing page, with all quotes paraphrased.
- Best for: teams whose primary need is branded link shortening with QR as an occasional add-on, organizations needing SOC 2 Type 2 compliance for link management, and companies already deep in the Bitly ecosystem. Page-1's verdict table lists it as best for "Link shortening (not QR)".
