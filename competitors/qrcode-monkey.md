Free for static QR codes only; QRCode Monkey has no paid plans of its own. Anything paid (dynamic codes, analytics, JPG/PNG downloads) requires a membership on QR Code Generator Pro, its sibling product, which the head-to-head article prices at ~$10/month with annual billing only.

## Free Tier

Unlimited free static QR codes with no signup, no expiry, and no scan limits; free SVG vector download; no ads on scans. JPG and PNG downloads are gated behind a QR Code Generator Pro membership, and dynamic codes/analytics are not offered at all.

## Pros

- Static QR codes are genuinely free: no signup, no expiry, no scan limits, no trial.
- Design customization (logos, colors, shapes, dot patterns) is unusually strong for a free tool.
- Free SVG vector export is print-quality and usable in professional layout software.
- Produces a one-off static code in under a minute.
- Does not inject ads on scans.
- Some Trustpilot reviewers report static codes that have kept working for years.

## Cons

- Creates static codes only — the destination is permanently encoded and cannot be edited after printing.
- No analytics or scan tracking of any kind (who scanned, when, or where).
- JPG and PNG downloads require a QR Code Generator Pro membership; only SVG export is free.
- Clicking to create a dynamic QR code redirects to QR Code Generator Pro, a sibling product with 9,220 Trustpilot reviews averaging 1.5 stars.
- No multi-PDF, audio QR, or vCard management — static URL-style codes only.
- No agency features: no client reports, no white-label, no UTM tracking, no print templates.

## Key Features

- Free static QR code generation with no signup, no expiry, and no scan limits
- Design customization: logos, colors, shapes, dot patterns
- Free print-quality SVG vector download
- No ads injected on scans

## Watch-outs

Checkable facts: static-only; JPG/PNG export is paywalled behind QR Code Generator Pro; the on-site dynamic-code option redirects to QR Code Generator Pro (same owner, Egoditor GmbH, since February 2024).

Pageloot frames this upgrade path as a "funnel" into a 1.5-star platform with a 14-day trial trap and surprise billing, and assigns QRCode Monkey a "Medium" trial-trap risk on its own scale. It paraphrases Trustpilot reviewers who printed "free" codes that later deactivated (one calling it an "absolute scam"), while noting those complaints center on users who unknowingly created dynamic codes through the QR Code Generator Pro funnel — plain static codes are reported to keep working.

## Notes

- **Ownership** (stated in all three articles, sourced to PitchBook): QRCode Monkey GmbH was acquired by Egoditor GmbH in February 2024; Egoditor operates QR Code Generator Pro and is owned by Bitly. The head-to-head gives the chain as QRCode Monkey → Egoditor → Bitly, while the roundup phrases it QRCode Monkey → Egoditor → QR Code Generator Pro → Bitly; the head-to-head version is used here since QR Code Generator Pro is Egoditor's product, not an owner.
- Headquartered in Dresden, Germany (per the head-to-head article).
- **Source bias:** all three articles are published by Pageloot, a direct competitor. Pageloot's subjective framing includes calling it "one of the best free static QR code generators on the web", "Reddit's free-static darling", and "a perfectly fine choice" for one-off codes — recommended alongside Pageloot's own free tool — while claiming it "now serves as a lead-generation tool" for QR Code Generator Pro and advising readers not to follow its upgrade path.
- Pageloot also claims millions of users have created codes with it.
