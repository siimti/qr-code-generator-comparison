## Free Tier

Free static QR codes only (per Pageloot's Reddit-roundup feature matrix). The 14-day free trial creates dynamic QR codes by default without clearly flagging that they deactivate after 14 days, and ads are shown on trial scans (same matrix). Reactivating deactivated trial codes requires the $120/year Starter plan, with no refund.

## Pros

- The core technology is functional — dynamic codes, scan analytics, and customization work.
- SOC2, CCPA, and GDPR compliance documentation is available on the enterprise tier through Bitly infrastructure.
- SSO/SAML is available on the enterprise tier.
- Enterprise clients praise its premium support and dedicated account management in reviews.
- Native option for teams already using Bitly link management, with QR codes in the same dashboard and analytics.
- Established market presence — part of Bitly, est. 2013 per Pageloot's comparison table.

## Cons

- The free trial creates dynamic QR codes by default that deactivate after 14 days — often discovered after materials are printed — with the $120/year no-refund Starter plan as the only reactivation path.
- 1.5-star Trustpilot average across ~9,200 reviews (9,194 per the dedicated head-to-head; 9,220 elsewhere), the lowest rating in Pageloot's comparisons.
- Annual billing is forced on Advanced (~$178/yr) and Professional plans; monthly billing exists only on Starter and was only recently added.
- Strict no-refund policy, with reviewers across multiple platforms reporting refund denials; some users disputed charges through their banks.
- Non-enterprise support is slow — reviewers report 3-7 day response times and template replies; support runs through Bitly Customer Support.
- No agency features at any price tier (no shareable client reports, white-label dashboard, UTM tracking, or custom table views), and data export is limited to scan statistics with no migration tools.

## Key Features

- Dynamic QR codes with scan analytics and customization (Pageloot's articles concede the core technology is functional)
- 16 dynamic QR code types
- Free static QR codes
- Multi-user seats (2-5 depending on plan)
- Landing pages in 31 languages; dashboard/app in 6 languages
- Basic folders for organization
- PDF and image file upload as QR (single PDF per code); Instagram print template only
- API (per Pageloot's feature matrix)
- Basic affiliate program
- Enterprise tier: SOC2/CCPA/GDPR compliance documentation and SSO/SAML via Bitly

## Watch-outs

- **Trial:** dynamic-by-default trial codes deactivate after 14 days without clear upfront warning; reactivation requires a $120+/year no-refund subscription.
- **Unclear checkout:** reviewers report the checkout flow is unclear about billing frequency, producing unexpected annual charges.
- **Auto-renewal:** subscriptions auto-renew, and cancellation requires 30-day advance written (email) notice before the billing cycle ends.
- **No exit path:** no export or migration tools beyond scan statistics.
- **Ads on trial:** ads are shown on trial scans (per Pageloot's Reddit-roundup matrix).

## Notes

**Source caveat:** all five source articles are published by Pageloot, a direct competitor, and all review quotes are paraphrased by Pageloot rather than verbatim.

**Ownership (checkable):** operated by Egoditor GmbH (Germany), part of Bitly (its support page references "Bitly Customer Support"). Egoditor acquired QRCode Monkey in February 2024 (sourced to PitchBook), whose dynamic-code upgrade path redirects into this platform. Related domains are the-qrcode-generator.com (now part of Uniqode's ecosystem) and my.qr-code-generator.com. As a German company, EU consumer protection laws apply for European customers.

**Data conflicts:** resolved toward the dedicated head-to-head ("Best QR Code Generator Pro Alternative", verified April 2026) — Trustpilot count 9,194 vs 9,220 in the four other articles, and "monthly billing recently added on Starter only" vs the QRCode Monkey article's "annual billing only".

**Pageloot's subjective framing (attributed, not endorsed):** "the technology works — the business model is the problem". Pageloot calls this "the tool Reddit warns about most" and notes Uniqode's competing roundup ranks it #1 despite the rating. Pageloot's roundup verdicts advise all buyers outside the enterprise/Bitly use cases — especially marketing teams, agencies, and anyone printing codes — to avoid it.

**Pageloot-paraphrased review anecdotes:** Pageloot relays reviews describing dynamic codes deactivating at trial expiry (an agency's retail-chain codes, wedding-invitation codes, a nonprofit's event code), a teacher charged $120 for 2 codes, and slow or automated-only support — sourced to Trustpilot, G2, and Capterra.
