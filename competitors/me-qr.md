## Pros

- Offers genuinely free dynamic QR codes that stay active, which most competitors charge for from day one.
- Widest QR-type breadth in the comparisons at 46+ types (vs Pageloot's 15).
- Built-in ME-Ticket event ticketing that no other tool in the comparisons offers.
- Includes a built-in in-app QR scanner, which Pageloot lacks.
- Supports bulk creation via CSV upload, Google Analytics integration, 28 landing-page languages, and claims GDPR compliance.
- Monthly billing is available, and Trustpilot reviewers praise ease of use.

## Cons

- An ad-filled interstitial page appears when codes are scanned on both the free plan and the paid Starter plan ($5.75–9/month), where only 1 code per account is ad-free.
- Making all codes ad-free requires the $15/month plan ($180/year).
- QR codes are not standalone — every scan routes through Me-QR servers, creating downtime risk, added latency, and privacy concerns.
- Multiple reviewers report cancellation difficulties, auto-renewal charges without warning, and hidden email-only support.
- No agency features (shareable client reports, white-label dashboard, built-in UTM tracking, print templates) and no multi-PDF or audio (MP3) QR codes.
- No lifetime deal — recurring subscriptions only, with no permanent code ownership.

## Key Features

- 46+ QR code types
- Free dynamic QR codes (ad-supported)
- ME-Ticket built-in event ticketing
- Built-in in-app QR scanner
- Bulk creation via CSV upload
- Google Analytics integration
- Landing pages in 28 languages
- Basic analytics and customization
- Claimed GDPR compliance
- Unlimited codes per plan (per the Reddit-roundup feature matrix)

## Watch-outs

- **Ads on scans up to $9/mo:** ads are injected on the free plan and paid plans up to $9/month, with only 1 ad-free code on the lowest paid tier; fully ad-free costs $15/month.
- **Redirect dependency:** codes depend on Me-QR's redirect servers, so server downtime breaks them.
- **Billing complaints:** reviewers (paraphrased by Pageloot from G2 and Trustpilot) report codes that worked cleanly at first later showing an ad "jumppage," no renewal warnings, auto-charges, email-only support, and escalating price increases that are hard to escape once codes are on printed materials.
- Pageloot's comparison tables rate Me-QR's trial-trap risk as **Medium**.
