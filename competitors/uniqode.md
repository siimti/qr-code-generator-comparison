Four QR plans from $9/month (Essential) to $399/month (Business+), all billed annually only with no monthly option — $108 to $4,788 per year paid upfront. Essential ($9/mo, annual only) is the lowest plan (owner-confirmed 2026-07-07; a live-web reading of "$5/mo" was incorrect and has been reverted).

## Free Tier

No ongoing free dynamic plan. There is a 14-day free trial for QR codes (no credit card required); after the trial, dynamic codes become inactive. A free static QR code generator is available.

## Pros

- Rated 4.8/5 on G2 across 507 reviews, with reviewers praising ease of use, customization options, and customer support.
- Holds SOC 2 Type 2, GDPR, HIPAA, and ISO 27001 certifications — the only tool in the sources' comparison matrix with the full compliance stack.
- Smart Rules redirect QR codes conditionally by time of day, device type, geographic location, or scan count.
- Offers a full digital business card product with two-way contact sharing, Apple/Google Wallet integration, and CRM sync alongside QR management.
- Native HubSpot and Salesforce integrations, 700+ apps via Zapier, and built-in lead capture forms with CRM sync.
- Guaranteed 99.99% uptime SLA and 24/7 support, with phone support on Plus plans and above.

## Cons

- All QR plans are billed annually only with no monthly option, requiring $108–$4,788 upfront per year.
- Steep tier jumps with no intermediate options: more than 15 codes forces $9 Essential to $49 Core, team access requires $99 Plus (3 users), and more than 3 users requires $399 Business+.
- No free tier — only a 14-day trial, after which dynamic codes become inactive.
- Analytics retention is capped below the top tier (60 days Essential, 90 Core, 180 Plus); lifetime retention requires Business+ at $399/month.
- No refunds on the current billing cycle and no lifetime deal option.
- English-only landing pages, dashboard, and support.

## Key Features

- SOC 2 Type 2, GDPR, HIPAA, and ISO 27001 certifications
- Smart Rules: conditional QR redirects by time of day, device type, location, or scan count
- Digital business cards with two-way contact sharing, Apple/Google Wallet integration, and CRM sync
- Built-in lead capture forms with CRM sync; Linkpages (multi-link landing pages)
- Native HubSpot and Salesforce integrations plus 700+ apps via Zapier
- Detailed scan analytics with GPS-level tracking on higher tiers (city-level geo, not state-level)
- Password-protected QR codes; custom domain (Core and up); SSO/SAML on enterprise tiers; anomalous scan detection
- 15+ dynamic QR code types; Meta Pixel and Google Analytics integration
- Public API
- White-label dashboard (Business+ tier only)
- 99.99% uptime SLA; 24/7 support with phone support on Plus and above
- 30% affiliate commissions

## Watch-outs

- **Annual billing only** on all QR plans (minimum $108/year); no refunds on the current billing cycle, so switchers must time cancellation before annual renewal.
- **14-day trial** after which dynamic codes become inactive — no ongoing free tier.
- **Steep all-or-nothing tier ladder** ($9 → $49 → $99 → $399/mo) with no intermediate options.
- **Analytics retention capped at 60–180 days** below the $399/mo tier; **white-label only at Business+**.
- File-based QR codes limited to single PDFs and images (no multi-PDF or audio/MP3 codes).

## Notes

- Both dedicated source articles are published by Pageloot, a direct competitor, though both explicitly concede Uniqode is a good product and the head-to-head includes a "When Uniqode is the better choice" section.
- Company facts per sources: formerly Beaconstac, headquartered in New York, founded 2012, serves 50,000+ businesses including Nestlé, Marriott, and Toyota; also operates The QR Code Generator (TQRCG) at the-qrcode-generator.com as a separate freemium product.
- Pageloot's framing: it calls the compliance stack "the strongest in the QR code category," positions Uniqode as "the enterprise QR platform" vs Pageloot "the agency QR platform," says enterprise compliance costs are "unnecessary overhead for agencies," claims Uniqode lacks agency features (shareable client reports, built-in UTM, print templates), claims Pageloot saves agencies $500–4,000/year, and says Uniqode is "the only option that does both" QR and digital business cards well.
- **Source conflict:** the head-to-head calls Smart Rules "a Uniqode-only feature," but Pageloot's own roundup feature matrix and text credit QR Tiger with conditional routing too — so "Uniqode-only" is not treated as fact here.
- Pageloot also claims Uniqode's own "5 Best" roundup ranks QR Code Generator Pro (1.5/5 on Trustpilot, 9,220 reviews) as its #1 pick.
- The Reddit roundup reports sentiment as "great but pricey" with a real-usage price floor around $49/mo, rates trial-trap risk low, and calls it "the right answer — full stop" when procurement requires certifications. A paraphrased Trustpilot reviewer said pricing increased over time and features were moved to higher tiers, reducing value for smaller teams.
